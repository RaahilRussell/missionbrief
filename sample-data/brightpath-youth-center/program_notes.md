# Program Notes — May 2026

Internal notes captured by program staff during the May reporting period. These are
the raw, messy notes a director would normally have to read through by hand before
writing a board update.

## Highlights

- The After-School Program hit its highest single-day attendance of the year on
  May 25 (48 youth). Word-of-mouth referrals from current families are driving growth.
- The STEM workshop series wrapped its spring cohort. 82% of enrolled youth completed
  all six sessions, up from 71% last cohort.
- Two mentoring matches that had stalled in April were successfully re-paired this month.
- A local employer (Local First Bank) committed $5,000 and offered to host a summer
  career day for older youth.

## Risks & Blockers

- **Staffing gap:** We missed the grant milestone to hire a bilingual family support
  coordinator by May 15. Two finalists declined offers over salary. This is now blocking
  expansion of family support intake.
- **Space constraint:** After-school attendance is approaching room capacity (55 seats).
  We may have to waitlist youth in June if growth continues.
- **Data gap:** Volunteer hours for the May 6 and May 13 family support sessions were
  logged on paper and may be undercounted.
- **Cash flow:** The second grant tranche from Cedar Valley is not expected until July,
  which tightens June payroll.

## Board Asks

- Approve a part-time contractor to cover family support intake until the coordinator
  role is filled.
- Decision needed on whether to cap after-school enrollment or add a second session.
- Guidance on a reserve policy given the July cash-flow gap.

## Looking Ahead

Summer programming planning is underway. We expect higher demand in July and August and
are coordinating with two partner schools on referrals.
